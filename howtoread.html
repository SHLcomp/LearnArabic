    To learn the letters you need to know the shape and the sounds of the letters.

    Click HERE for a video tutorial if needed.

    Notice the notes under each card, these are helpful as they give brief explanation helping you to memorise them.

    After being familiar with the letter shapes, you can use this sentence to memorise the alphabet:

    أبجد هوز حطي كلمن سعفص قرشت ثخذ ضغظ

  • You might notice that Arabic letters are written from right to left, unlike English and most languages.
  • You will notice NUMBERS written to represent how some letters sound like, you'll understand why by the end of the first lesson of this course. Good luck!
  • Unlike most languages, Arabic letters have names that are not just the sound they make...and the reason after this is quite interesting. If you want to know why click HERE.

    First lesson:

    The Alphabet (Al-Abjadiyya)

  • What is Al-Abjadiyya?

    • Comes from the word Abjad which is simply the first 5 letters ordered in an order called Al-Abjadiyya. The same word existed in the introduction of this page, specifically in the Arabic sentence wich lists the alphabet in the Al-Abjadiyya order. Need more help? Here.

  • Note:

    • you can scroll down in some cards.

    There are 28 letters in the Arabic alphabet, only 3 letters are vowels, so we will need "Tashkeel" to know how to read properly, which you'll learn in the next lesson.
